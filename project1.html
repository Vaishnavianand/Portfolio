Website Migration
CMS Teamsite to AEM Migration.
** As this was a client-side project which is still in the last delivering phase , I am not at the liberty to disclose the intricate details or use website images and screenshots. **

Migrating from the existing CMS (Content Management System) to AEM (Adobe Experience Manager) for an American timeshare company in order to deliver an enhanced digital experience for the users.

BACKGROUND

The AEM migration project was a client-side project with a US based MNC - Travel + Leisure Co. for their RCI division which is a premier vacation exchange company. The current CMS of the company was a website where a user can sign up to buy a membership and then book vacations by choosing the desired resorts or hotels along with the option of modifying all activities and amenities that the user requires.

ROLE

I worked as a UI developer in a team of 5 developers and 2 UX designers at Cognizant Technology and Solutions.

TOOLS

Visual Studio Code, BitBucket, JIRA, InVision Design

CODING

HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, React JS, Node JS

THE SITUATION

The CMS website of the business had its limitations of implementing functionalities which could give the user an upgraded experience on the website. Hence the migration to Adobe Experience Manager.
But the authors of the business have been using the same website since quite a few years and are used to it, so it was decided, that the fields and overall design will be exactly like the preceding version, but with better functionalities.

UNDERSTANDING THE TASK

The main motive of the development team was to transfer the existing website to AEM in a step-by-step migration process of :
  1. Design
  2. Functionalities
  3. Content
After the completion of the above, move on to the security and deployment challenges and resolve them efficiently.

METHODOLOGY

ACTION PROCESS

What did I do ?
  • I started off by understanding the flow of the existing website in terms of the user journey beginning from the landing page , onto the searching of resorts/hotels/retreats, booking the desired stay with all the desired modifications in terms of activities and ending with checkout and payment.
  • Before starting the development coding, I worked with the UI/UX designers of our clients who gave a detailed mockup of the design and user journey (using Invision as the design tool) which had to be implemented while migrating the website.
  • In terms of coding I contributed to each component the development team made by using HTML, CSS, React JS and Node JS to implement the functionalities and design of the website.
  • After the coding of each component I worked with the testers to fix all of the accessibility issues of the website so that it could also be used by people with impaired vision.
  • I collaborated my work with the other developers of my team on BitBucket and updated the business team of the client via Jira and Confluence.
