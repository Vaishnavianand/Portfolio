Effects Of Device Parameters On The Mobility Of An Organic Thin Film Transistor
Paper publication in IEEE India Council International Conference 2020.

BACKGROUND

I published a paper with IEEE 17th India Council International Conference (INDICON) 2020 titled “Effects Of Device Parameters On The Mobility Of An Organic Thin Film Transistor” under the guidance of Dr Rajesh Agarwal. My work majorly focused on researching about organic nature of OTFTs, conducting various simulations on TCAD Silvaco Software and extracting graphs on Tonyplot. I worked with 5 other authors on the paper.

TITLE

Effects Of Device Parameters On The Mobility Of An Organic Thin Film Transistor

PUBLISHER

Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE)

CITATION

S. Sharma, P. Deep, R. Tandon, V. Anand, P. S. Kumar and R. Agarwal, "Effects of Electrical and Physical Parameters on Mobility of Organic Thin Film Transistor," 2020 IEEE 17th India Council International Conference (INDICON), 2020, pp. 1-5, doi: 10.1109/INDICON49873.2020.9342258.

ABSTRACT

In the present work, the mobility of an organic thin-film transistor is evaluated and compared both in linear and saturation regions for various critical electrical and physical parameters. The varying parameters include channel length, oxide layer thickness, bandgap, electron affinity etc. The work has been carried for both p-type and n-type semiconductors using the simulation tool from TCAD. A Pentacene/Silicon-dioxide top contact bottom gate geometry has been adopted for the present study but can be extended to bottom contact also. Relating to these results, conclusions are drawn which might be useful for industrial applications.
